Steak


A steak, also sometimes called "beef steak", is a meat generally sliced across the muscle fibers, potentially including a bone. It is normally grilled, though it can also be pan-fried. Steak can also be cooked in sauce, such as in steak and kidney pie, or minced and formed into patties, such as hamburgers.

Ingredients

  • Thick cut steak
  • Butter
  • Olive oil
  • Garlic
  • Fresh Thyme

Method

  1. Bring steak to room temperature.
  2. Pat dry and season the steak generously with salt and pepper - this helps form that amazing crust we all know and love about great steaks.
  3. Get your skillet SMOKING HOT before putting the steak in - again, for the crust
  4. Wait 2.5 minutes and then flip the steak
  5. After another minute add the butter, thyme and garlic.
  6. Lower to medium heat and baste the steak with the melted butter.
  7. When the steak reaches internal temperature of 52 degrees take off skillet.
  8. Allow steak to rest for 5-10 minutes before serving.
  9. Serve and enjoy with family and friends!
